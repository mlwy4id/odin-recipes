Sate Ayam

Indonesian authentic chicken satay

Sate is basically a meat or sometimes offals skewered on bamboo sticks and then grilled. They are usually served with sauce. Many people think that when it comes to sate, the sauce that comes with it is usually peanut based.

Ingredients

  • 750 gr chicken thighs
  • 9 cloves garlic
  • 50 gr palm sugar
  • 3 cm tumeric
  • 1 lemon (take the juices)
  • to taste Salt
  • 125 gr peanut
  • 250 ml coconut milk
  • 1 cup mixture of tamarind and hot water
  • 1 onion
  • 5 candlenuts
  • 4 Thai chili
  • 5 tbsp oil to saute
  • 10 tbsp sweet soy sauce
  • 1 cube palm sugar
  • 1 tsp white pepper powder
  • 1/4 tsp garlic salt
  • 1/5 cup hot water

Steps

  1. Make a paste from garlic, turmeric, palm sugar, salt, and lime leaves (3). Marinate the diced chicken with the paste and add lemon juice.
  2. Marinate it for 2-3 hours, then arrange the marinated chicken into the skewer. The more you put the chicken in the skewer the less satay you will get.
  3. Make the paste from the onion, garlic, Thai chili, candlenuts, and salt. Grind the peanuts.
  4. Preheat the saucepan with oil then saute the onion-garlic paste. Once it wilted add the peanut ground.
  5. Then add the coconut milk, tamarind water, and sweet soy sauce. Cook it till it boiled and became like a porridge (after 15-20 minutes the liquid will decrease).
  6. Mix all the barbecue sauce ingredients then dip the skewered chicken and grill (I use toaster or you can use the grilled) it.
  7. Turn the skewered chicken on both sides, then dip it in the peanuts sauce and grill it again till it cooks.
  8. Chicken satay is ready to serve with rice or lontong (plain rice cooked using banana leaves), peanuts sauce, added topping Thai chili and acar (Indonesian pickle).
